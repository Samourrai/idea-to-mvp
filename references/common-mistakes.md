# Common Ideation Mistakes

## Mistake 1: Solution-First Thinking
**Pattern:** "I want to build an app that does X"
**Problem:** Starts with the solution, not the problem. The solution may be solving the wrong thing.
**Fix:** Ask "What problem are you solving?" and "Who has that problem?" before discussing the solution at all.

## Mistake 2: The Aggregator Fantasy
**Pattern:** "I'll build a marketplace / aggregator / platform that connects X with Y"
**Problem:** Marketplaces require solving a chicken-and-egg problem. They're extremely hard for solo founders.
**Fix:** Ask which side of the market they'll serve first. Can they provide value to just one side without the other?

## Mistake 3: Competing on Features (Not Problem)
**Pattern:** "My version will be like [competitor] but with better UX and also does A, B, and C"
**Problem:** Feature lists don't make businesses. You need a reason someone would switch.
**Fix:** Ask "Who are the people [competitor] is failing, and why are they being failed?"

## Mistake 4: "I'll worry about monetization later"
**Pattern:** Get users first, figure out money later.
**Problem:** Price is part of product. Free users have different expectations than paid customers.
**Fix:** Ask them to name a price right now. If they can't, that's the real problem to solve.

## Mistake 5: Confusing Interest with Validation
**Pattern:** "100 people signed up to my waitlist!"
**Problem:** Email signups are nearly free. They measure curiosity, not willingness to pay.
**Fix:** Only treat these as validation: pre-payment, repeat usage, and unprompted referrals.

## Mistake 6: Building for "Everyone"
**Pattern:** "This could be used by anyone who..."
**Problem:** If it's for everyone, it's for no one. Marketing is impossible. Messaging is impossible.
**Fix:** Force specificity. Ask them to name an actual person they could call right now who has this problem.

## Mistake 7: Stealth Mode
**Pattern:** "I can't tell people about my idea yet because someone will steal it"
**Problem:** Ideas are worthless without execution. Staying stealth means no validation.
**Fix:** "Can you name a single example of a startup idea that was stolen by someone who heard it before launch?"

## Mistake 8: The Perfect Launch Fallacy
**Pattern:** "I want to launch when the product is really polished"
**Problem:** Delaying launch delays learning. You'll be wrong about what "polished" means anyway.
**Fix:** "What's the minimum that would embarrass you just a little bit? Ship that."

## Mistake 9: Building for Imaginary Customers
**Pattern:** The founder imagines who the customer is, but has never spoken to one.
**Problem:** Everything downstream will be wrong.
**Fix:** Hard stop. "Before we continue — have you talked to 5 people who have this problem? Let's plan those conversations first."

## Mistake 10: Founder-Market Fit Blindness
**Pattern:** Chasing an idea in a space they have no connection to.
**Problem:** Low likelihood of success. Slow learning. No credibility with early customers.
**Fix:** "Why are you the person to solve this? What gives you an advantage here over anyone else?"
